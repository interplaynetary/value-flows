hREA User Guide ​

Welcome to hREA ​

This guide helps you understand and use hREA (Holochain/REA) for coordinating economic activities. Whether you're managing a supply chain, tracking production processes, or organizing community resource sharing, this guide will help you get started.

Getting Started ​

What is hREA? ​

hREA is a system for tracking economic activities like:

  • Resource Management: Inventory tracking and resource allocation
  • Supply Chain Coordination: Managing product flows from producer to consumer
  • Service Delivery: Tracking service commitments and delivery
  • Community Sharing: Organizing shared resources and collaborative projects
  • Project Management: Coordinating work and resource allocation

Key Concepts ​

Economic Agents ​

  • People: Individuals participating in economic activities
  • Organizations: Companies, non-profits, cooperatives
  • Groups: Teams, departments, project groups

Economic Resources ​

  • Tangible Goods: Physical products, materials, equipment
  • Services: Labor, expertise, consulting
  • Digital Assets: Software, data, intellectual property

Economic Events ​

  • Transfers: Moving resources between agents
  • Production: Creating new resources through processes
  • Consumption: Using resources in activities
  • Services: Delivering value to others

Understanding Economic Concepts ​

REA Accounting Model ​

hREA is based on the REA (Resources, Events, Agents) accounting model:

  • Resources: Economic goods and services
  • Events: Economic activities affecting resources
  • Agents: People and organizations involved in economic activities

Double-Entry Economic Accounting ​

Every economic activity has balanced entries:

  • When resources move from Agent A to Agent B
  • Both sides of the transaction are recorded
  • Complete audit trail of all economic flows

ValueFlows Standards ​

hREA implements the ValueFlows specification for:

  • Standardized Vocabulary: Consistent terminology
  • Interoperability: Works with other ValueFlows-compatible systems
  • Semantic Clarity: Unambiguous economic relationships

Working with Agents ​

Creating an Agent Profile ​

Agents represent people and organizations in the economic network.

Creating a Person ​

  1. Basic Information

    • Name: "Alice Johnson"
    • Type: "Person"
    • Contact information (optional)

  2. Additional Details

    • Profile picture (optional)
    • Skills or specializations
    • Notes about background or expertise

Creating an Organization ​

  1. Organization Details

    • Name: "Green Foods Cooperative"
    • Type: "Organization"
    • Description of activities

  2. Classification

    • Business type: "cooperative", "retail", "organic-food"
    • Certifications: "organic-certified", "fair-trade"

  3. Contact Information

    • Website, phone number
    • Physical address

Agent Relationships ​

Team Memberships ​

  • Join organizations as a member
  • Participate in projects and groups
  • Define roles and permissions

Trust Networks ​

  • Build trust relationships with other agents
  • Create reputation through reliable commitments
  • Develop long-term economic relationships

Managing Resources ​

What Are Resources? ​

Resources are any economic goods, services, or items that have value:

  • Physical Products: Food, electronics, materials
  • Equipment: Tools, machinery, vehicles
  • Digital Assets: Software, data, designs
  • Services: Consulting, labor, expertise

Creating Resources ​

Product Resources ​

  1. Basic Information

    • Name: "Organic Tomatoes"
    • Category: "Food", "Vegetables", "Organic"
    • Unit: "kg", "pieces", "boxes"

  2. Quality Specifications

    • Grade or quality level
    • Certification requirements
    • Physical specifications

  3. Inventory Tracking

    • Current quantity available
    • Location information
    • Batch/lot numbers for traceability

Equipment Resources ​

  1. Equipment Details

    • Name: "Industrial Mixer Model X"
    • Serial number or ID
    • Maintenance schedule

  2. Usage Terms

    • Available for sharing or rental
    • Usage conditions
    • Insurance requirements

Service Resources ​

  1. Service Definition

    • Type of service offered
    • Skills and expertise level
    • Time unit (hourly, daily, project-based)

  2. Availability

    • Schedule and working hours
    • Geographic location or remote work
    • Minimum booking requirements

Resource Tracking ​

Inventory Management ​

  • Track quantities of physical resources
  • Monitor resource locations
  • Set minimum stock levels for automatic reordering

Quality Control ​

  • Record quality grades and certifications
  • Track inspection results
  • Monitor expiration dates for perishable items

Resource Relationships ​

  • Group related resources together
  • Create product bundles
  • Track component relationships

Tracking Economic Events ​

What Are Economic Events? ​

Economic events are specific activities that affect resources:

  • Transfer Events: Moving resources between agents
  • Production Events: Creating new resources
  • Consumption Events: Using resources in processes
  • Service Events: Delivering services

Creating Transfer Events ​

Product Delivery ​

  1. Select Resources

    • Choose the specific product or batch
    • Verify quantity and quality

  2. Set Transaction Details

    • From: "Green Farms" (provider)
    • To: "Local Market" (receiver)
    • Quantity: "100 kg"
    • Location: "Market Warehouse"

  3. Record the Event

    • Date and time of delivery
    • Delivery notes or special conditions
    • Quality inspection results

Service Delivery ​

  1. Define Service

    • Type of service provided
    • Duration or scope of work
    • Location or remote setup

  2. Record Completion

    • Service completion date
    • Customer satisfaction
    • Any issues or special notes

Creating Production Events ​

Manufacturing Production ​

  1. Process Setup

    • Select manufacturing process
    • Input materials and quantities
    • Expected output

  2. Record Production

    • Actual resources consumed
    • Products created
    • Quality control results
    • Time and labor used

Agricultural Production ​

  1. Growing Process

    • Seeds, water, and inputs used
    • Growing conditions and timeline
    • Harvest results

  2. Post-Harvest Processing

    • Cleaning, sorting, packaging
    • Quality grades achieved
    • Storage requirements

Creating Processes ​

What Are Processes? ​

Processes are organized workflows that transform resources:

  • Manufacturing Processes: Converting raw materials to finished goods
  • Service Processes: Delivering value through services
  • Agricultural Processes: Growing and harvesting activities
  • Administrative Processes: Planning and coordination activities

Process Planning ​

Define Process Objectives ​

  • What the process will accomplish
  • Resources needed
  • Timeline and schedule
  • Quality requirements

Create Process Template ​

  1. Basic Information

    • Process name: "Weekly Manufacturing Run"
    • Duration: 8 hours
    • Location: "Production Line A"

  2. Resource Requirements

    • Input materials and quantities
    • Equipment and tools needed
    • Personnel and skills required

  3. Expected Outputs

    • Products to be produced
    • Quality standards
    • Byproducts and waste materials

Process Execution ​

Start the Process ​

  1. Verify Resources

    • Check all required materials are available
    • Confirm equipment is operational
    • Verify personnel are present

  2. Record Process Start

    • Actual start time
    • Any deviations from plan
    • Environmental conditions

Track Progress ​

  1. Input Consumption

    • Record materials used
    • Track consumption rates
    • Monitor for variances

  2. Quality Control

    • Perform quality checks at key stages
    • Record inspection results
    • Address any issues immediately

Complete the Process ​

  1. Final Output

    • Record products produced
    • Final quality inspection
    • Clean-up and maintenance

  2. Process Evaluation

    • Compare actual vs. planned results
    • Identify efficiency improvements
    • Document lessons learned

Making and Fulfilling Commitments ​

What Are Commitments? ​

Commitments are promises to perform economic actions:

  • Delivery Commitments: Promise to deliver products or services
  • Production Commitments: Promise to create resources
  • Usage Commitments: Promise to use resources in specific ways

Creating Commitments ​

Product Delivery Commitments ​

  1. Commitment Details

    • What: "1000 units of Product X"
    • Who: From "Supplier A" to "Customer B"
    • When: "By March 15, 2024"

  2. Terms and Conditions

    • Quality specifications
    • Delivery location
    • Payment terms

  3. Legal Reference

    • Contract number or agreement reference
    • Terms and conditions
    • Dispute resolution process

Service Commitments ​

  1. Service Definition

    • Type of service
    • Scope of work
    • Time commitment

  2. Performance Standards

    • Quality metrics
    • Response times
    • Reporting requirements

Fulfilling Commitments ​

Track Progress ​

  • Monitor commitment deadlines
  • Record fulfillment events
  • Handle delays or issues

Complete Fulfillment ​

  1. Verify Delivery

    • Confirm quantity and quality
    • Record completion date
    • Get confirmation from receiver

  2. Update Records

    • Mark commitment as fulfilled
    • Update inventory records
    • Generate completion reports

Working with Plans ​

What Are Plans? ​

Plans organize multiple economic activities toward common goals:

  • Production Plans: Schedule manufacturing activities
  • Project Plans: Coordinate complex projects
  • Supply Chain Plans: Plan resource flows across networks

Creating Plans ​

Define Plan Objectives ​

  • Overall goals and objectives
  • Timeline and milestones
  • Resource requirements

Structure the Plan ​

  1. Phases or Stages

    • Break down into manageable phases
    • Define dependencies between phases
    • Set milestones and deadlines

  2. Resource Allocation

    • Assign resources to activities
    • Balance resource needs and availability
    • Plan for contingencies

Execute and Monitor ​

  • Track progress against plan
  • Adjust for variances and changes
  • Report on plan completion
